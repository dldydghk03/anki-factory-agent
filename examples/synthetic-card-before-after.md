# Synthetic Card Before and After

These examples are written from scratch. They are not copied from a private
lecture, drive file, or generated deck.

## Example 1: Weak Cloze Target

Before:

```text
All PPGLs are treated as potentially metastatic. SDHB mutation increases this
risk to more than {{c1::40}}%.
```

Problem:

The number can be useful, but the more important recall target is the mutation
that changes risk interpretation.

After:

```text
All PPGLs are treated as having metastatic potential. A {{c1::SDHB mutation}}
is a high-risk genetic clue because it is associated with a much higher risk of
metastatic disease.
```

## Example 2: Missing Foundation Concept

Before:

```text
The old rule of tens says bilateral, extra-adrenal, and metastatic PPGL are
each about 10%.
```

Problem:

The card uses terms that may not have been introduced yet.

After:

```text
Before using PPGL frequency rules, define the axes:
bilateral means tumors in both adrenal glands, extra-adrenal means the tumor is
outside the adrenal gland, and metastatic means spread to a site where
chromaffin tissue is not normally present.
```

## Example 3: Awkward Source Phrasing

Before:

```text
When meeting an adrenal incidentaloma, PPGL should first be excluded.
```

After:

```text
When an adrenal incidentaloma is found, it is important to check first whether
it is PPGL, because unrecognized catecholamine secretion can increase
perioperative cardiovascular risk.
```
